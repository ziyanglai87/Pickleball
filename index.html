Rating Engine
How Ratings Work
1. Team rating
A doubles team rating is the average of the two players’ current ratings before the game.
Example: 3.20 + 3.40 ÷ 2 = 3.30 team rating.
2. Expected result
The dashboard compares both team ratings and estimates expected point share using a logistic curve.
Equal teams ≈ 50/50. Stronger team expects more points.
3. Actual result
Actual performance is based on points won, not just win/loss.
Winning 11–2 is more dominant than 11–9.
4. Rating change
Rating moves based on actual point share minus expected point share, multiplied by the K factor.
Outperform expectation → rating rises. Underperform → rating falls.
5. Provisional effect
Newer players move faster because their ratings are less certain. As matches increase, ratings stabilise.
First 20 games are treated as calibration period.
6. Recalculation
When a match is edited, deleted or restored, the dashboard rebuilds ratings from the match history.
This keeps the rating ladder internally consistent.
